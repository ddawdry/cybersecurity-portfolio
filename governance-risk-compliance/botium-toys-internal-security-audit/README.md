# Botium Toys Internal Audit

## Overview
This project was completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate. The activity focused on conducting an internal security audit using the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) to identify risks, review security controls, and evaluate compliance practices.

The project includes:
- Risk assessment analysis
- Security controls assessment
- Compliance review
- Security recommendations

---

## Disclaimer
Botium Toys is a fictional company created for educational purposes as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate.

---

## Scenario Summary
Botium Toys is a small toy company with both physical and online operations. As the company expanded its online business and international customer base, concerns were raised regarding security controls, asset management, and regulatory compliance. An internal audit was conducted to assess the organization’s current security posture and identify areas requiring improvement.

---

## Objectives
The objectives of this audit were to:
- Identify security risks and vulnerabilities
- Assess existing administrative, technical, and physical controls
- Review compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC standards
- Recommend improvements to strengthen security and compliance

---

## Frameworks and Standards Used
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF)
- PCI DSS
- GDPR
- SOC Controls

---

## Key Findings
The audit identified multiple security and compliance concerns, including:
- Lack of encryption for customer payment data
- Missing intrusion detection system (IDS)
- No disaster recovery or backup procedures
- Weak password policy enforcement
- Excessive employee access to sensitive information
- Missing least privilege and separation of duties controls

This audit identified multiple missing controls and compliance gaps that increase organisational risk. The recommendations gave aim to improve Botium Toys’ overall security posture, strengthen protection of sensitive data, and support regulatory compliance.
